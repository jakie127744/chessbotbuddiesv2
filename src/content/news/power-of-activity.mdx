<h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-white mb-2">
  The Power of Activity: A Lesson in Pressure
</h3>
<p className="mb-4 italic text-zinc-400">
  Every chess game is a conversation between two players. In this game, White
  shows us how to use active pieces to create a powerful attack. Let’s follow
  the journey of the pieces as they move across the board.
</p>

<hr className="border-white/10 my-8" />

<h4 className="text-lg font-bold text-sky-400 mb-4">The Opening Battle</h4>
<p className="mb-4">
  White begins with <strong>1. d4</strong>. This move claims space in the center
  and opens a path for the Queen and Bishop. Black replies with{" "}
  <strong>1... Nf6</strong>, a flexible move that develops a piece and stops
  White from placing another pawn in the center immediately. White continues
  with <strong>2. Nf3</strong>, bringing out a Knight toward the middle. Black
  plays <strong>2... g6</strong>, preparing to place the Bishop on a long
  diagonal where it can watch the whole board.
</p>
<p className="mb-4">
  White develops the Bishop to a sneaky square with <strong>3. Bg5</strong>.
  This puts pressure on Black’s Knight. Black follows the plan with{" "}
  <strong>3... Bg7</strong>, creating a strong "fianchetto" house for the
  Bishop. White brings out the second Knight with <strong>4. Nbd2</strong>. By
  placing the Knight here, White keeps the path clear for the pawns to move
  forward later. Black finishes the first goal of the opening by playing{" "}
  <strong>4... O-O</strong>, putting the King into a safe castle.
</p>

<h4 className="text-lg font-bold text-sky-400 mb-4">Fighting for the Center</h4>
<p className="mb-4">
  White now plays <strong>5. e4</strong>, taking control of the very center of
  the board. Black fights back immediately with <strong>5... d5</strong>. This
  challenges White's pawn and asks a question: will White move forward or take?
  White decides to trade with <strong>6. exd5</strong>. Black brings the Queen
  out early with <strong>6... Qxd5</strong> to recapture. Usually, it is risky
  to bring the Queen out so soon, and White shows why by playing{" "}
  <strong>7. Bc4</strong>. This move develops a piece and attacks the Black
  Queen at the same time.
</p>
<p className="mb-4">
  Black moves the Queen to safety with <strong>7... Qd6</strong>. White finishes
  development on the Kingside with <strong>8. O-O</strong>. Now both Kings are
  tucked away. Black plays <strong>8... a6</strong>, preparing to expand on the
  other side of the board. White plays <strong>9. c3</strong>, building a solid
  wall of pawns to support the center.
</p>

<h4 className="text-lg font-bold text-sky-400 mb-4">Expanding the Horizons</h4>
<p className="mb-4">
  Black continues the plan with <strong>9... b5</strong>, attacking White’s
  Bishop and gaining space. The Bishop retreats to a good square with{" "}
  <strong>10. Bd3</strong>. Black brings the final minor piece into the game
  with <strong>10... Bb7</strong>. Both sides have developed their pieces well.
  White now tries to break open the side of the board with{" "}
  <strong>11. a4</strong>. Black pushes past with <strong>11... b4</strong>,
  refusing to let the lines open easily.
</p>
<p className="mb-4">
  White finds a very active square for the Knight: <strong>12. Nc4</strong>.
  Once again, the Black Queen is under fire and must move. She goes to{" "}
  <strong>12... Qd5</strong>. White brings the Rook to the center with{" "}
  <strong>13. Re1</strong>. This Rook is now looking directly at Black’s center
  pawns. Black develops the last Knight with <strong>13... Nc6</strong>.
</p>

<h4 className="text-lg font-bold text-sky-400 mb-4">The Attack Begins</h4>
<p className="mb-4">
  White centralizes the Queen with <strong>14. Qe2</strong>, lining up behind
  the Knight. Black moves the Rook to <strong>14... Rfe8</strong> to protect the
  center. White decides it is time to leap forward with{" "}
  <strong>15. Nce5</strong>. This Knight is now very deep in Black's territory.
  Black tries to break White’s pawn structure with <strong>15... bxc3</strong>.
</p>
<p className="mb-4">
  Instead of taking back, White makes a bold move: <strong>16. Bc4!</strong>{" "}
  This ignores the pawn and goes straight for an attack on the Black Queen and
  the weak f7-pawn near the King. Black counters by attacking White’s Queen with{" "}
  <strong>16... Nxd4</strong>. This is a very complicated moment! White moves
  the Queen back safely to <strong>17. Qf1</strong>. Now, Black must deal with
  the threat to their own King. Black trades Knights with{" "}
  <strong>17... Nxf3+</strong>, and White recaptures with{" "}
  <strong>18. gxf3</strong>.
</p>
<p className="mb-4">
  White’s pawns are a bit messy now, but the pieces are very aggressive. Black
  moves the Queen to <strong>18... Qc5</strong>, but White ignores the danger
  and strikes with <strong>19. Bxf7+</strong>. White has broken into the King’s
  castle! The Black King hides in the corner with <strong>19... Kh8</strong>.
</p>

<h4 className="text-lg font-bold text-sky-400 mb-4">Total Pressure</h4>
<p className="mb-4">
  White plays <strong>20. b4</strong>, distracting the Black Queen. After{" "}
  <strong>20... Qb6</strong>, White pushes again with <strong>21. a5</strong>.
  White is using the pawns like a fence to push Black’s pieces away from the
  defense. Black moves to <strong>21... Qb5</strong>. White brings the Bishop
  back into the action with <strong>22. Bc4</strong>. The Bishop is a hero in
  this game, constantly switching between attack and defense.
</p>
<p className="mb-4">
  Black captures a pawn with <strong>22... Qxb4</strong>, but this move takes
  the Queen away from the King's defense. White seizes the chance and swings the
  Queen over with <strong>23. Qh3</strong>. This move creates a scary threat
  against the Black King. Black tries to block with <strong>23... Nh5</strong>.
</p>

<h4 className="text-lg font-bold text-sky-400 mb-4">The Final Blows</h4>
<p className="mb-4">
  White begins a forcing sequence with <strong>24. Nf7+</strong>. The King must
  move back to <strong>24... Kg8</strong>. Now White plays{" "}
  <strong>25. Qe6</strong>. This move creates a "discovered attack" threat. If
  White moves the Knight, the Queen will be attacking the King. Black tries to
  create some room with <strong>25... h6</strong>, but it is too late.
</p>
<p className="mb-4">
  White jumps back with <strong>26. Ne5+</strong>, checking the King. The King
  returns to the corner with <strong>26... Kh8</strong>. White captures a key
  pawn with <strong>27. Qxg6</strong>. Black is running out of moves and tries{" "}
  <strong>27... Bxf3</strong> to create a distraction. White ignores it and
  brings the Bishop to the center with <strong>28. Bd3</strong>. This move
  threatens checkmate on the next turn!
</p>
<p className="mb-4">
  Black tries to run with <strong>28... Kg8</strong>, but White keeps the
  pressure on with <strong>29. Qh7+</strong>. The King moves to{" "}
  <strong>29... Kf8</strong>. Finally, White plays <strong>30. Nd7+</strong>.
  Black resigns because mate is unavoidable. A brutal attack where every white
  piece joined the party!
</p>
