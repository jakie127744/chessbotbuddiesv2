### Opening: A Quiet Start Turns Sharp

The game begins with **1.d4 d5 2.c4 e6**, heading into a Queen’s Gambit structure. With **3.Nf3 Nf6 4.g3**, White chooses a flexible setup, planning to fianchetto the bishop.

Black immediately grabs material with **4…dxc4**, holding onto the pawn. After **5.Bg2**, Black plays **5…a6**, preparing queenside expansion. White castles with **6.O-O**, and Black boldly continues with **6…b5**, defending the extra pawn.

Now White strikes. **7.Ne5** centralizes the knight and points toward f7. Black pushes it back with **7…Nd5**, but White responds powerfully with **8.e4**, building a strong center.

Black retreats with **8…Nf6**, and White immediately challenges the queenside structure using **9.a4**. After **9…Bb7**, White opens lines with **10.axb5 axb5**.

### Early Material Imbalance

White now plays the surprising **11.Rxa8**, sacrificing a rook to damage Black’s coordination. Black accepts with **11…Bxa8**, but Black’s queenside is now loose and underdeveloped.

### The Second Sacrifice

### The King Walk Begins

White opens the center immediately with **20.dxc5**, tearing away Black’s pawn cover. Black grabs material with **20…Nxe5**, but this allows a powerful check.

With **21.Qd6+**, White forces the king forward. Black steps to **21…Kf6**, beginning a dangerous journey. White calmly improves a piece with **22.Nc3**, keeping threats alive.

Black retreats with **22…Qd8**, but White jumps in with **23.Nd5+**, another forcing move. Black has no choice but **23…Kf5**, marching deeper into enemy territory.

### No Way Back

White now throws everything into the attack with **24.g4+**, opening more lines. Black captures with **24…Kxg4**, but the king is now completely exposed.

White strikes again with **25.Qxe5**, removing a key defender. Black responds with **25…exd5**, but it is far too late to consolidate.

With **26.f3+**, White delivers the final blow. Black’s king runs to **26…Kxh4**, but there is no escape left.
