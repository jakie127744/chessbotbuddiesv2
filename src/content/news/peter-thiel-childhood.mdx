<h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-white mt-8 mb-4">The Grand Strategy</h3>
<p className="mb-4">
    Thiel took the lessons of the chess board directly to Silicon Valley. In his famous book <em>Zero to One</em>, he quotes chess legend Capablanca: "You must study the endgame before everything else." Thiel realized that most startups fail because they don't plan their endgame. They make moves without knowing where they want to land.
</p>

 <div className="bg-sky-900/20 border border-sky-500/30 p-6 rounded-xl my-8">
    <h4 className="text-lg font-bold text-sky-400 mb-2">Did You Know?</h4>
    <p className="text-zinc-300">
        Thiel believes that competition is for losers. In chess, if you strive for equality, you draw. To win, you must do something different—something your opponent doesn't expect. He applied this "contrarian" thinking to business, investing in crazy ideas like electric cars and space travel when no one else would.
    </p>
</div>

<h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-white mt-8 mb-4">Planning the Future</h3>
<p className="mb-4">
    Peter Thiel's career proves that chess isn't just a game of moving wood. It's a way of looking at the future. By calculating variations and valuing his assets correctly, he turned a National Master title into a billion-dollar empire.
</p>
